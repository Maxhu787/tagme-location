# Tagme

September launch
Work from 22:30pm ~ 2:00am on every weekend
and anytime during weekdays.

---

### **Phase 1: Initial Setup & Development (Jan 28 – Apr 24)**

- **Research (Jan 25 – Jan 27)**:

  - Research best practices for setting up an Expo app and integrating Supabase.
  - Look into setting up Stripe for payments and possible monetization models.

- **Week 1-4 (Jan 28 – Feb 24)**:

  - Set up Expo app and Supabase integration.
  - Implement Supabase Auth for user login and registration.
  - Design the database schema for real-time location tracking.

- **Week 5-8 (Feb 25 – Mar 23)**:

  - Build core app functionality for real-time location sharing and user profiles.
  - Test database read/write intervals for scalability.
  - Research Stripe integration and how it can fit into your app's monetization strategy (e.g., subscriptions, in-app purchases).

- **Week 9-12 (Mar 24 – Apr 21)**:
  - Enhance UI/UX with animations and transitions.
  - Optimize backend queries to reduce latency.
  - Implement crash/error tracking (e.g., Sentry).

---

### **Phase 2: Advanced Features & Internal Testing (Apr 25 – Jul 10)**

- **Research (Apr 22 – Apr 24)**:

  - Research advanced features (e.g., push notifications, location-based alerts).
  - Explore monetization strategies, like adding premium subscriptions or pay-per-use features with Stripe.

- **Week 13-16 (Apr 25 – May 19)**:

  - Add advanced features such as notifications and location-based actions.
  - Integrate app analytics for tracking performance and user behavior.
  - Conduct internal testing with small groups.

- **Week 17-20 (May 20 – Jun 16)**:

  - Refine the app based on internal feedback. (open a google form)
  - Resolve bugs and ensure the app works across various screen sizes.
  - Test Stripe integration for payment workflows (e.g., checkout flow, subscription handling).

- **Week 21-22 (Jun 17 – Jul 7)**:
  - Finalize MVP features and polish the UI.
  - Conduct final performance optimizations.

---

### **Phase 3: Beta Testing & Pre-launch (Jul 11 – Aug 21)**

- **Research (Jul 8 – Jul 10)**:

  - Research promotional strategies for your app’s beta launch.
  - Finalize your monetization model using Stripe and confirm how you’ll integrate payments.

- **Week 23-26 (Jul 11 – Aug 4)**:

  - Launch a private beta with select users.
  - Collect feedback on usability and performance.
  - Refine monetization models based on user feedback (e.g., in-app purchases, subscription tiers).

- **Week 27-28 (Aug 5 – Aug 18)**:
  - Finalize app store assets (screenshots, app description, promotional video).
  - Set up marketing materials and pre-launch landing page.

---

### **Phase 4: Marketing & Final Preparation (Aug 22 – Sept 8)**

- **Research (Aug 19 – Aug 21)**:

  - Research additional monetization features (e.g., limited-time offers, discounts).
  - Explore app launch strategies to increase visibility.

- **Week 29-30 (Aug 22 – Sept 1)**:

  - Ramp up marketing efforts (social media, influencers, email list).
  - Conduct final stress tests and server performance checks.
  - Implement any last-minute monetization tweaks (e.g., adding a premium tier or special offers).

- **Week 31-32 (Sept 2 – Sept 8)**:
  - Submit the app to the App Store and Google Play.
  - Finalize launch marketing and prepare for the official launch date.

---

### **Phase 5: Official Launch & Post-launch Support (Sept 9)**

- Launch your app globally with a coordinated marketing campaign.
- Monitor performance and resolve any immediate issues.
- Actively engage with users, gather feedback, and plan for regular updates.

---

### **Monetization Research & Implementation (Throughout the Project)**

- **Initial Research (Jan 25 – Jan 27)**:

  - Research how Stripe can be used for in-app purchases, subscriptions, or pay-per-use services.
  - Explore potential monetization models (e.g., freemium model, premium subscriptions, one-time payments).

- **Ongoing Implementation (Throughout Phases 1-4)**:
  - Integrate Stripe for handling user payments and manage subscriptions.
  - Regularly review feedback from beta and soft launch users to adjust monetization strategies.
